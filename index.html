Samantha Trinkler

Newcastle (AUS) based graphic designer, illustrator and artist.
Samantha Trinkler’s practice is at the meeting point between three realms; traditional, digital and graphic design. Her work includes editorial, illustration, motion design and website/app development and design. Keen on experimentation and with a particular interest towards scientific-based design, she enjoys merging the old and the new to create new visual codes.

She is a part of the Swiss-based multidisciplinary studio Cave Canem, founded in 2023.

Hydro-hegemony

2022
378 pages, 23 × 29,5 × 3 cm
Concept and design: Samantha Trinkler, Nathanaël Vianin, Lara Zettl

Visit the website

Water is life, yet water is inherently political. As cities expand, urban populations are increasingly in competition with farmers and industry for access to water. Hydro-hegemony consists of showcasing water conflicts from 3000 BC right until the 21st century, demonstrating how water is the lifeblood of ecosystems; global environmental change, particularly climate change, and is further intensifying competition among rival uses and users, leading to conflicts and war.

Legatum Digitalis

2022
Diploma project
Our lives have become increasingly digitalised. Despite growing concerns about the data we leave behind, only a small number of people are actively planning their own digital legacy, or know how to. Legatum Digitalis challenges the lack of awareness about the digital footprint we leave behind us after death and their inheritance, and speculates on future inheritance processes whilst providing a contextual, legal, and statistical overview.

Placid regular

2021
Type design


Placid is a typography created based on effective readability and emerging calm technology theories.

Calm technology theories propose that technology should seamlessly integrate into our lives, enhancing our experiences without causing unnecessary distractions or stress. It emphasizes designing technology to be unobtrusive and intuitive, allowing users to remain calm and focused on their tasks or surroundings.
